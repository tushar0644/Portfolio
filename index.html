Case Study 01

Movie Ticket Booking App

Redesigning the cinema booking experience for mobile-first users

View Prototype

Duration

3 Weeks

Role

Solo Designer

Platform

Mobile App

Screens

15+

The Problem

Traditional movie booking apps suffer from cluttered interfaces, complicated seat selection processes, and poor mobile optimization. Users often abandon bookings due to friction in the ticket selection flow and lack of visual clarity regarding seat availability and cinema layout.

"How might we streamline the movie ticket booking process to reduce booking time by 50% while maintaining a premium, cinema-like experience?"

Key Challenges
  • Complex seat selection UI
  • Unclear pricing breakdown
  • Poor mobile responsiveness
  • Lack of visual feedback

Design Process

1
Research

Competitor analysis of BookMyShow, Paytm, and INOX apps

2
User Flows

Mapped optimal booking journey from browse to confirmation

3
Wireframing

Low-fi sketches focusing on thumb-zone navigation

4
Visual Design

Dark theme with high contrast for cinema ambiance

The Solution

Intuitive Seat Selection

Interactive seat map with color-coded availability, zoom functionality, and clear pricing tiers (Standard, Premium, VIP).

Quick Booking Flow

Reduced steps from 5 to 3 with smart defaults and persistent cart across sessions.

Digital Ticket UI

Apple Wallet-style digital tickets with QR codes, showtime reminders, and easy sharing.

Impact & Learnings

40%

Reduction in booking steps

WCAG 2.1

AA Accessibility compliance

Dark Mode

Premium cinema aesthetic

Case Study 02

PM Internship Portal

Government-scale internship discovery platform for first-time job seekers

View Prototype

Duration

4 Weeks

Role

UI/UX Designer

Platform

Mobile-first Web

Scale

Government

The Problem

Government internship portals often prioritize information density over usability, creating barriers for first-time job seekers from diverse educational backgrounds. Users struggle with complex filtering, unclear application statuses, and overwhelming listing pages that lack mobile optimization.

"How might we design an inclusive internship discovery experience that serves users with varying digital literacy levels while handling high-volume government-scale traffic?"

Target Users
  • College Students

    Seeking first internship experience

  • Recent Graduates

    Transitioning to professional roles

  • Rural Applicants

    Limited digital literacy, mobile-only access

Design Process

1
User Research

Interviews with 12 students; identified pain points in existing portals

2
Information Arch

Simplified navigation; reduced menu items from 12 to 5

3
Accessibility

High contrast modes, screen reader support, simplified language

4
Prototyping

Interactive Figma prototype with 20+ screens and user flows

The Solution

Smart Filtering

Location, stipend range, and duration filters with visual chips and one-tap clearing. Reduced filter interaction time by 60%.

Save & Track

Bookmark internships and track application status with color-coded badges (Applied, Under Review, Shortlisted).

Simplified Cards

Internship cards showing only essential info: Role, Stipend, Location, Deadline. Reduced cognitive load for quick scanning.

Mobile-First

Designed for 320px screens first, ensuring accessibility on budget smartphones

Accessibility

WCAG 2.1 AA compliant with 4.5:1 contrast ratios and scalable text

Performance

Optimized for 2G/3G networks with skeleton screens and lazy loading

Impact & Outcomes

60%

Faster task completion in user testing

5→3

Reduced main navigation categories

100%

Screen reader compatibility
